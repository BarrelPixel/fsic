## Extracted Notes

### Four-Engine Model
*Sources: Overview Page 1–2; Master Plan Pages 9–11*

FSIC operates through a coordinated, phased four-engine architecture:

- **Talent Engine** — Drives employer-aligned training, micro-credential academies, dual-enrollment STEM pathways, industry-embedded learning, teacher upskilling, and a Regional Talent Marketplace. First engine deployed. (Master Plan Page 9–10)
- **Innovation Engine** — Powers challenge labs, rapid prototyping studios, hackathons/innovation sprints, applied research partnerships, and dual-use technology development. Region's first civilian-facing applied innovation hub. (Master Plan Page 10)
- **Market Engine** — Accelerates supplier readiness bootcamps, SBIR/STTR navigation, certification support (AS9100, ITAR, CMMC), startup acceleration, contract matchmaking, and venture funding pathways. (Master Plan Pages 10–11)
- **Civic Engine** — Engages the community through STEM nights, arts-and-space integration, public exhibitions, regional storytelling/branding, and transparent governance. Builds identity, trust, and civic legitimacy. (Master Plan Page 11)

Each engine reinforces the others, creating a coordinated regional ecosystem. (Overview Page 2)

### 25-Year Economic Impact Figures
*Sources: Overview Page 1; Master Plan Pages 7–8*

Independent 25-year analysis projects FSIC will generate:

| Metric | Range |
|--------|-------|
| Total economic output | $10.2–$12.4 billion |
| Job-years supported | 74,500–79,500 |
| New wages (FL workers) | $1.35–$1.55 billion |
| Fiscal revenue (local & state) | $690–$910 million |
| External funding potential (federal, state, private, philanthropic) | $800 million–$1.56 billion |

Additional highlights:
- Jobs span construction, operations, research, training, and supply chain (Master Plan Page 7)
- Wages expand access to higher-wage career pathways in aerospace, engineering, advanced manufacturing, and technology (Master Plan Page 7)
- FSIC accelerates Deering Park's development absorption timeline from 20–25 years to 10–13 years (Master Plan Page 8)
- Three-scenario analysis: Baseline (No FSIC), Basic FSIC (FSIC 1.0), Extended FSIC (FSIC 2.0) (Master Plan Page 8)

### Phased Deployment Timeline (Year 1–5)
*Source: Master Plan Pages 13–14, 32–34*

| Phase | Timeframe | Focus |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Phase 1: Foundation & Talent Engine Launch | Year 1 | Establish governance, hire leadership, launch micro-credentials, dual-enrollment, Regional Talent Marketplace, employer advisory councils |
| Phase 2: Innovation Engine Activation | Years 1–2 | Launch challenge labs, rapid prototyping studio, university/federal research partnerships, innovation events |
| Phase 3: Market Engine Deployment | Years 2–3 | Supplier-readiness bootcamps, SBIR/STTR navigation, certification support, startup acceleration, contract matchmaking |
| Phase 4: Civic Engine Expansion | Years 2–4 | Public exhibitions, arts-and-space programs, community STEM nights, regional storytelling, transparent reporting |
| Phase 5: Full Ecosystem Integration | Years 3–5 | Integrate all four engines, expand facilities at Deering Park, pursue major federal/state funding, formalize long-term employer partnerships |

Year-by-year milestones are detailed on Master Plan Pages 33–34.

### Governance Structure Highlights
*Source: Master Plan Pages 15–17*

- **Board of Directors:** Independent governing body with community leaders, industry representatives, education/workforce leaders, economic development partners, philanthropic/civic leaders, non-voting ex officio roles. (Page 15)
- **Board Independence:** Independent board chair, term limits, conflict-of-interest policies, balanced sector representation, clear separation between governance and operations. (Pages 15–16)
- **Executive Leadership:** CEO with directors for each engine plus Director of Operations and Finance. (Page 16)
- **Advisory Councils:** Employer, Education & Workforce, Innovation & Research, Community & Civic, Small Business & Supplier. (Page 16)
- **Board of Advisors:** High-level guidance from influential community leaders, senior executives, philanthropic influencers, university/research partners, entrepreneurs. (Pages 16–17)
- **Standing Committees:** Finance & Audit, Governance & Nominating, Programs & Impact, Development & Partnerships. (Page 17)
- **Founder Transition:** Structured plan — founder serves as unpaid CEO during startup, transitions to advisory/emeritus role, board assumes full governance authority. (Page 17)

### Long-Term Vision (2032–2040)
*Sources: Overview Page 3; Master Plan Pages 36–38*

Three major pillars beyond the 2026–2031 master plan:

1. **Multi-University Consortium** — Multiple universities with permanent physical presence at Deering Park, shared research labs, joint faculty appointments, cross-institutional degree pathways in aerospace, robotics, AI, materials, and systems engineering. Modeled on Houston Spaceport, NSF Engines hubs. (Master Plan Page 36–37)

2. **Multi-Purpose Auditorium & Performing Arts Center** — Dual-role facility for large-scale convenings, industry summits, STEM showcases, and community performing arts (concerts, theater, dance, film festivals, school performances, cultural events). Financed through Space Florida tools. (Master Plan Page 37)

3. **Landmark Campus at Deering Park** — Signature architectural identity including multi-university research complex, performing arts center, innovation labs, Talent Engine pavilion, public plazas, green spaces, and community gathering areas. Positions New Smyrna Beach as leader in space-industry innovation and education. (Master Plan Page 38)

### Strategic Context
*Sources: Master Plan Pages 4–6*

- The Space Coast is the busiest launch corridor in the world (Page 4)
- Southeast Volusia risks becoming a commuter community to Brevard and Orlando without coordinated action (Page 4)
- Federal policy is shifting toward place-based innovation ecosystems (CHIPS & Science Act, Build Back Better, EDA Tech Hubs, NSF Engines) (Page 4)
- Deering Park: 10,000+ new homes, 10M+ sq ft industrial/commercial space — "once-in-a-generation" catalyst (Pages 4, 6)
- No civilian-facing innovation hub exists on the Space Coast (Page 4)
