## THE STRATEGIC MOMENT

**Headline:**
The Question Is Not Whether the Space Coast Will Grow.
It Is Whether Southeast Volusia Will Participate in That Growth.

**Body:**
Florida's space economy is expanding rapidly — driven by commercial launch activity, advanced manufacturing, aerospace engineering, and emerging technologies.

Without coordination, growth concentrates elsewhere.

The Florida Space Innovation Center (FSIC) ensures that Southeast Volusia is not a spectator to that growth, but an active participant — building the talent, infrastructure, and partnerships required to compete in a global innovation economy.

FSIC is designed to align education, industry, and community in one coordinated regional platform.

## THE FOUR-ENGINE MODEL

**Headline:** One Coordinated Platform. Four Integrated Engines.

**Body:** FSIC operates through a structured, phased model designed for early activation and long-term scale.

**Cards:**
- Talent Engine — Drives employer-aligned training, micro-credential academies, dual-enrollment pathways, and industry-embedded learning — transforming individuals into skilled talent.
- Innovation Engine — Powers challenge labs, applied research partnerships, prototyping studios, and university collaboration — transforming knowledge into innovation.
- Market Engine — Accelerates supplier readiness, startup development, SBIR/STTR navigation, and venture pathways — transforming opportunity into market success.
- Civic Engine — Engages the broader community through STEM programming, exhibitions, arts-and-space initiatives, and public events — transforming innovation into shared identity.

**Body close:** Each engine reinforces the others. Together, they create a coordinated regional ecosystem.

## PHASED DEPLOYMENT

**Headline:** Start Small. Deliver Early Wins. Scale with Readiness.
